Leonardo Davinci

About

Leonardo da Vinci was a polymath of the Italian Renaissance whose genius spanned a wide range of disciplines, including art, science, technology, and engineering. He is widely considered to be one of the greatest painters of all time, and his work continues to fascinate and inspire people around the world. Leonardo was born in Vinci, Italy, in 1452. He was the illegitimate son of an important Florentine lawyer and a peasant woman. He was raised by his father and his father's family, and he showed an early aptitude for art and science.

At the age of 14, Leonardo was apprenticed to Andrea del Verrocchio, a leading Florentine sculptor, painter, and goldsmith. Verrocchio's workshop was a hub of creativity, and Leonardo was exposed to a wide range of artistic and technical skills. One of Leonardo's earliest works is the angel in Verrocchio's sculpture of the Baptism of Christ. Leonardo's angel is so lifelike and expressive that it is said to have caused Verrocchio to declare that he would never sculpt again. Leonardo's two most famous paintings are the Mona Lisa and The Last Supper. The Mona Lisa is a portrait of a woman with an enigmatic smile, and it is considered to be one of the greatest masterpieces of Western art.
